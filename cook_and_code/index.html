Orlando Cook and Code

What we're about

Orlando Cook and Code is a private meetup that gives participants a good excuse to work on their side projects. It welcomes people of all backgrounds, experiences and ages to come together and share the joys of cooking, building and socializing in a casual environment.

Putting together your own Cook and Code

What to Bring

  • Something they'll work on. People in the past have brought their side projects, blogs, online classes. Anything for self improvement.
  • Laptop/Tablet or digital device
  • Food. Snacks and drinks are most welcome.

Chef prep list

If you're planning on cooking, we have the following tips:

  • Prep all your vegetables ahead of time. Cut them up the night before, so you can spend morme time working on your project.
  • Marinate and thaw all meats ahead of time.
  • Choose simple recipes. Time flies fast.
  • Use fancy tech if you got em. We love the InstantPot.

Scheduling Template

  • 12:00pm - 12:30pm - Introductions

    We all meet together. People state their goals they seek to accomplish during the coding hours during the cook and code

  • 12:30pm - 01:15pm - Cooking

    The chef roles prepare the food. Others start coding and working on their side projects.

  • 01:15 - 3:45pm - Working on Side Projects

    Everybody works on their project at this time. We encourage some collaboration, but keeping the social chatter light so people can stay focused.

  • 03:45pm - 04:45pm - Eating

    Food is served. We all discuss how we did, and seeing what we can do advance their projects.

  • 04:45 - Close - Clean up
